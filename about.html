About

I'm an engineer and builder at heart. I've spent 18+ years shipping software — from distributed systems to GenAI products. This project is my attempt to contribute to Huntington's Disease research using the tools I know best: data, AI, and code.

Applying the power of agentic AI to hard problems is something I want to do. HD is one of the hardest — and the tools are finally here. Gene therapies are showing real results (Nature, 2025). AI is designing millions of candidate compounds (Drug Target Review). Digital twins are accelerating trials (Newswire, 2026). I wanted to see what autonomous agents and open-source tools could contribute.

So I built autonomous research agents that scan PubMed, track clinical trials, generate drug repurposing hypotheses, and publish what they find — openly, with full methodology, and honest about limitations. Everything runs on an NVIDIA Jetson in my home office. Zero cloud cost.

This is an experiment in applying AI to a real problem. Our hypotheses are starting points for researchers to evaluate, not conclusions. We publish what works and what doesn't, because that's how good research operates.

Everything is open source. MIT-licensed. Built on public data from PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, and HDBuzz.

The art of the possible starts with someone deciding to try.

The Soul of This Project

Five principles we operate by. Inspired by people who changed how we think about technology.

rocket_launch
First Principles

Reason from fundamentals, not by analogy to what's been done before. "People's thinking process is too bound by convention." Break the problem down to its atoms and rebuild.

Inspired by Elon Musk

bolt
Speed of Light

The only constraint should be physics, not process. How fast can it be done — and why aren't we doing it that fast? No task is beneath us. Urgency is real when people are waiting.

Inspired by Jensen Huang

science
Autonomous Experimentation

Ship the loop, not the slide deck. Set up the agent, define the reward signal, let it run 100 experiments overnight. The human role shifts from experimenter to experimental designer.

Inspired by Andrej Karpathy

school
Democratize

AI is the new electricity — make it accessible to everyone, not just experts. If 8 million people can learn ML through a free course, imagine what open HD research tools can unlock.

Inspired by Andrew Ng

favorite
Benevolent Technology

"Tools don't have independent values — their values are human values." Every tool we've ever created, we wanted to use for good. That's the arc of civilization. Our technology reflects our values.

Inspired by Fei-Fei Li

Let's Work Together

This project is open to collaboration at every level. If any of the below resonates, reach out.

HD Researchers + Clinicians

Review our AI-generated hypotheses. Tell us what's promising and what's wrong. Your domain expertise is what turns computation into science. We'll credit you and co-publish.

AI/ML Engineers + Data Scientists

Run our agents with different models. Add new data sources. Build better hypothesis scoring. Fork, improve, PR — or build something entirely new on top of our data.

Organizations + Foundations

If you work in HD research, drug discovery, or health AI and want to explore how open-source tools can complement your work — let's talk. We're looking for partners, not customers.

Students + Educators

Use this as a teaching example of applied AI. Our experiment reports walk through every step. We're happy to guest-lecture, mentor, or help integrate this into coursework.
