Key Concepts to Understand

CAG Repeat Expansion

The HTT gene has a repeated CAG sequence. 36+ repeats cause HD. More repeats = earlier onset. Understanding this is foundational.

Somatic Expansion

CAG repeats grow longer in brain cells over time (somatic expansion). MSH3, FAN1, PMS1 are modifier genes. Hottest target area right now.

Mutant Huntingtin (mHTT)

The toxic protein produced by the mutated gene. Most therapies aim to lower mHTT levels or clear its aggregates.

Gene Therapy (ASO, AAV)

ASOs (antisense oligonucleotides) silence the gene. AAV (adeno-associated virus) delivers corrective genes. AMT-130 is an AAV therapy.

Drug Repurposing

Finding new uses for existing FDA-approved drugs. Faster path to patients because safety is already proven. This is what our AI agents do.

Neurofilament Light (NfL)

A biomarker in blood/CSF that indicates neuronal damage. Used to track HD progression and treatment response.

Digital Twins

AI-simulated patients used as controls in clinical trials. Could eliminate need for placebo arms with sham surgery.
