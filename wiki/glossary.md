# Glossary

Key HD concepts. For newcomers and for the chatbot's context.

**CAG Repeat Expansion** — The HTT gene has a repeated CAG sequence. 36+ repeats cause HD. More repeats = earlier onset.

**Somatic Expansion** — CAG repeats grow longer in brain cells over time. MSH3, FAN1, PMS1 are modifier genes that control this. Hottest research frontier.

**Mutant Huntingtin (mHTT)** — The toxic protein produced by the mutated HTT gene. Most therapies aim to lower mHTT levels or clear aggregates.

**Gene Therapy (AAV)** — Adeno-associated virus delivers corrective genes to the brain. AMT-130 is an AAV therapy showing 75% disease slowing.

**Antisense Oligonucleotide (ASO)** — Short DNA/RNA that silences the HTT gene. Tominersen (Roche) is an ASO therapy.

**Neurofilament Light (NfL)** — Blood/CSF biomarker indicating neuronal damage. Used to track HD progression and treatment response.

**cGAS-STING Pathway** — Immune signaling pathway activated by mitochondrial DNA release. Drives neuroinflammation in HD, AD, PD.

**Drug Repurposing** — Finding new uses for existing FDA-approved drugs. Faster path because safety is already proven.

**Digital Twin** — AI-simulated patient used as control in clinical trials. Unlearn is building these for HD.

**Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)** — Membrane protecting the brain. Most drugs can't cross it. Major challenge for HD therapeutics.

**PROTAC** — Targeted protein degradation technology. Arvinas is developing an oral PROTAC for HD that crosses the BBB.

**Autoresearch** — Karpathy's pattern: AI agent runs experiments autonomously overnight, keeps what improves the metric, discards the rest.

**RAG** — Retrieval-Augmented Generation. The chatbot searches our knowledge base before answering, so responses cite real papers.
